Honda Civic

otally redesigned last year, the 2023 Honda Civic carries over largely unchanged. That’s no bad thing though because the reinvented Civic sits firmly at the top of its class. The handsome styling houses an interior so roomy it feels closer to midsize than compact, and its well-tuned chassis and engines make even the base model fun to drive. Honda also piles on safety features, although getting the choicest gear in other areas means upgrading to higher-spec models. Some rivals do better in individual areas, like fuel economy or acceleration, but the Civic is a jack of all trades and a master of most.

Hyundai Elantra

The Elantra doesn’t dominate in any single area, but it does everything well and is undeniably value-packed. The Elantra stands near the top of its class in nearly every area, and dominates in several, like back seat room and fuel economy. Its overachieving interior is loaded with standard features and driver-assistance tech. And shoppers get a full range of models, including a fuel-sipping hybrid and a knockout Elantra N that may offer the most bang for the buck of any small performance car in today’s showrooms.

Kia Forte

The Kia Forte turns six years old as it enters 2023 and without major changes to the compact sedan. That’s because it hasn’t needed much change, apart from its mid-cycle refresh in 2020. Despite its age, it continues withstanding the test of time by proving that big value can still come in small packages without too much compromise and at a reasonable cost, especially with steep inflation and supply chain issues still lingering. The top GT can perform (at a more than acceptable $25,000) and it’s one of the few sedans that still comes with a third pedal.
